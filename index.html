01

What was the problem?

The B2B e-commerce platform was struggling with a rising number of unauthorized and counterfeit product listings. This not only degraded the trust of major brands but also impacted the overall customer experience and compliance standards. A highly scalable, automated system was required to flag these listings before they went live.

02

How did you approach it?

Instead of relying on manual moderators, I architected a Brand Authorization Platform using Java and Spring Boot microservices. To handle the high volume of daily listings efficiently, I utilized Elasticsearch for lightning-fast restricted keyword detection and built a rules engine that cross-references seller credentials with brand authorization registries.

03

What did you actually do?

I implemented the core backend microservices responsible for digesting product feeds and passing them through the Elasticsearch keyword analyzer. If a restricted brand keyword was detected, the system invoked a verification service. I also designed the database schema in PostgreSQL to manage authorization statuses and built the REST APIs consumed by the seller dashboard.

04

What was the result?

The Brand Authorization Platform successfully automated the moderation pipeline. By accurately detecting and blocking unauthorized products, we achieved a 40% drop in counterfeit listings. This restoration of trust directly correlated with a 30% increase in sales for genuine, authorized brand partners across the platform.
